## Interview 1 — [Nikil], [Tester], [Solo/Early stage]

**Date:** 2026-05-10
**How I found them:** college classmate

**Direct quotes:**

- "I pay for ChatGPT Plus but honestly I don't even know what the team plan costs"
- "We have 4 developers all on Cursor Pro — I just approved it without checking"
- "I'd use something like this if it took under 2 minutes"

**Most surprising thing they said:**
They had no idea GitHub Copilot had an Individual plan — they thought Business was the only paid option.

**What it changed about my design:**
I added a "Free plan entered" hint below $0 spend inputs so users know entering 0 is valid — they kept asking if they should skip free tools.

## Interview 2 — [Rahul], [training center], [Small Startup Team]

**Date:** 2026-05-10
**How I found them:** friend from internship group

**Direct quotes:**

"Bro I think our company is paying for the same AI feature three different times"
"Nobody really checks these subscriptions after the first month"
"If your tool can tell me what to cancel without breaking workflow, I’d actually use it"

**Most surprising thing they said:**
They were paying for both Cursor Pro and ChatGPT Team, but most of the team only used ChatGPT for basic prompts and rarely touched advanced features.

**What it changed about my design:**
I realized users don’t just want “save money” advice. They want confidence that removing or downgrading a tool won’t slow their work down. So I started thinking about adding short explanations like:

“Low risk downgrade”
“Likely overkill for current usage”
“Keep this if your team relies on advanced coding workflows”

## Interview 3 — [Aman], [Agency Owner], [Small Team]

**Date:** 2026-05-11
How I found them: LinkedIn connection

**Direct quotes:**

"I keep finding random AI charges on my card every month"
"Half the time I forget who even requested these subscriptions"
"If this gave me a clean breakdown instantly, I’d definitely try it"

**Most surprising thing they said:**
They were still paying for tools that ex-employees originally requested months ago.

**What it changed about my design:**
I realized the product should feel more like a “subscription health check” instead of just a calculator. I started making the audit output simpler and more visual so users can immediately spot waste without reading long explanations.
