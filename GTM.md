# Go To Market

## Who Exactly

Not "startups." The specific person is an engineering manager or CTO at a 5 to 30 person B2B SaaS company, Series A or bootstrapped, who personally approved buying AI tools for the team. They're paying $300 to $2,000 a month across 3 or 4 tools. They signed up for the tools when they were new and exciting and haven't revisited the plans since. They feel vaguely guilty about the AI bill but it's not painful enough to spend a Saturday researching alternatives.

That guilt is the entry point.

## What They Search Before Wanting This

I looked at what people actually type, not what sounds good:

- "cursor pro vs business plan"
- "is claude team plan worth it for small team"
- "github copilot business vs individual"
- "chatgpt team plan minimum seats"
- "reduce saas spend startup 2025"

These are comparison and justification searches. The person already paying and wondering if they made the right call.

## Where They Actually Are Online

- Hacker News — Show HN on weekday mornings, especially Tuesday and Wednesday
- r/ExperiencedDevs and r/SideProject on Reddit
- Indie Hackers — the weekly "what did you ship" thread
- Slack communities — Rands Leadership, Software Lead Weekly
- Twitter — following people like @levelsio, @marc_louvion, @t3dotgg who post about tool costs

## First 100 Users in 30 Days, Zero Budget

**Week 1:** Post "Show HN: I built a free AI spend audit for startups" on Hacker News at 9am EST on a Tuesday. A decent Show HN gets 300 to 1000 visits. The headline has to be about the user's problem, not the tool.

**Week 1-2:** Post a real audit result on Twitter. Not "check out my tool" — post the actual finding. "Ran an audit on a 4-person startup's AI tools. Found $340/mo in waste. Here's exactly where it was leaking →" with a link. This format gets reshared because it's useful information, not promotion.

**Week 2:** Cold DM 30 founders on Twitter who have tweeted about AI tool costs in the last 3 months. Search "cursor pricing" or "claude plan" on Twitter and find people complaining. DM them directly: "Saw you were looking at Cursor pricing — built something that might help, free to use, would love your feedback."

**Week 3:** Submit to newsletters that engineering managers actually read — TLDR, Pointer.io, Software Lead Weekly. These accept free tool submissions.

**Week 4:** Post in Indie Hackers "What I built this week" thread with a real savings example. IH founders are the exact target user.

## The Unfair Channel

Credex already has customers buying AI credits. Every existing Credex customer is a perfect SpendLens user — they already care about AI tool costs, they're already spending money on it. An email to the existing Credex customer list with "audit your full stack" is a distribution channel no competitor has.
